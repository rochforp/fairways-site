Since 2013, when Tiger Woods won five times during the first half of the season, golf fans have been locked in a kind of push-and-pull with the 14-time major champ. Is he injured? Does he have the yips? Has he lost his passion for the game? A back injury that arose towards the end of 2013 surfaced early in 2014 and ruled him out for a large bulk of the season. And even when he returned in time for the British Open and PGA Championship, it was clear Tiger wasn’t ready. 2015 was supposed to be a fresh slate for Tiger, but after withdrawing from the Farmers Insurance Open with an injury and questions lingering over his short game woes, people began to fear the worst: That maybe this was it. Golf’s new, Tiger-less reality that we should brace for. He started skipping tournaments he ordinarily played, and for vague, confusing reasons about ‘his game not feeling ready.’


There are still questions, of course. Will his body hold up? Can he win again? Will he ever win another major? The answers to all those remain to be seen. But if nothing else, Tiger — in his own mind at least — finally feels like Tiger again.
